Murad Hasan

I received my B.SC. in computer science from Brac University and am currently employed as a contractual lecturer at the same institution. I do research on computer vision and machine learning.

Under the supervision of MD. Golam Rabiul Alam and co-supervision of MD Tanzim Reza, I worked on image and video processing in my undergraduate thesis, which led me to research on state-of-the-art deep learning techniques. I also co-authored a conference paper in which I worked on several widely used convolutional neural network models.

From 2021 to 2022, I worked as an undergraduate teaching assistant, which allowed me to assist students during lab and consultation hours. I have been honored with the distinction of receiving the Vice Chancellor's Award, being named to the Dean's List, and being awarded a scholarship based on merit.

Research

I'm interested in computer vision, machine learning, and image processing. Much of my research is about human action recognition and visual question answering, where I develop models that include human pose estimation, facial features, and human motion.
